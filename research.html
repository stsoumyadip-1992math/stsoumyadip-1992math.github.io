Research

My research lies in Algebraic Topology, with a primary focus on Equivariant Rational Homotopy Theory and Topology of Complex Varieties and some specific part of Geometric Group Theory. Broadly speaking, I am interested in understanding how symmetries of topological spaces can be described using algebraic models and how these models can be applied to study spaces equipped with finite group actions.

My work combines ideas from Sullivan's rational homotopy theory, transformation groups, equivariant topology, study of topology of varieties over complex numbers and geometric group theory. A central theme of my research is the construction of explicit algebraic models that capture the equivariant topology of spaces and provide effective tools for computing topological invariants.

Current Research Projects

Equivariant Rational Homotopy Theory

I study rational models for spaces equipped with finite group actions, with particular emphasis on orbit-category methods and Sullivan models. My current work investigates the intrinsic formality of equivariant spaces such as complex projective spaces and Grassmannians.

Geometric Group Theory

I am also interested in problems related to mapping class groups and their associated simplicial complexes. My recent work includes the study of mapping tori and related questions in geometric topology.

Future Directions

  • Equivariant formality and intrinsic formality over disconnected orbit cateogries.
  • Rational models for homogeneous spaces.
  • Applications of equivariant Sullivan models.
  • Interactions between algebraic topology and geometric group theory.
  • Topology of varieties over complex numbers.
